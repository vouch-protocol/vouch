Watch a key stay out of reach.

A private key is the one thing that should never move. Vouch keeps it in place three ways: each device signs with a key it minted itself, a lost root rebuilds from shares that reveal nothing below their threshold, and a group of custodians can sign together without any one of them ever holding the whole key. All three run below, on the real credential shapes.

Every verdict here mirrors the shipped SDK modules vouch.fleet, vouch.recovery, and vouch.threshold. Available in every SDK: Python, TypeScript, Go, JVM, .NET, C, and Swift.

§ I

Cross-device enrollment and revocation

One identity across your devices, and revoking one you lost · vouch.fleet

Each device mints its own key locally, and the root identity signs a scoped, time-bound grant to that device's DID. The device signs its actions with its own key, chained under the grant, so the private key never travels between devices. A verifier ties the action back to the trusted root.

§ II

Root recovery

Rebuild the root from a threshold of Shamir shares · vouch.recovery

The root identity's seed splits into 5 shares, any 3 of which rebuild it exactly. Distribute them to guardians or separate locations. Fewer than the threshold combine to a share-length result, but not the original identity, and reveal nothing about it.

§ III

Threshold signing

Any threshold of custodians signs, the full key never exists whole · vouch.threshold

A key is split among 3 custodians so that any 2 of them can sign together, and the full private key never exists whole at any point, not even during signing. Whichever 2 sign, the result is the same standard Ed25519 signature over the same group public key.
