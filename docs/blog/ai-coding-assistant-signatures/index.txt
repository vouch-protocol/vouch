d:T5d9,<h3>The Pain Point</h3>
<p>You use GitHub Copilot or Cursor. You write 80% of the architecture, but specific blocks are just "Tab" completions.</p>
<p>To the world (and your boss/professor), it all looks the same.</p>
<ul><li><strong>Problem:</strong> If you can't prove which parts <em>you</em> thought of, you risk being labeled "just a prompter."</li>
<li><strong>Risk:</strong> Compliance teams might ban AI tools entirely because they can't distinguish "safe human code" from "AI hallucination."</li>
</ul>
<h3>The Solution: "Ghost Signatures" (Your Typing Fingerprint)</h3>
<p>The system works inside your IDE (VS Code/Cursor) and watches <em>how</em> code appears:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Human Mode:</strong> You type at 40-100 words per minute. You backspace. You pause to think.</li>
<li><strong>Coding Assistant Mode:</strong> Code appears instantly (1000+ chars in 10ms) or via "Tab" acceptance.</li>
</ul>
<p>It creates a signed map of your file:</p>
<p>> <em>"Lines 1-20: Human Authored (99% confidence)"</em></p>
<p>> <em>"Lines 21-30: AI Generated (Copilot)"</em></p>

<h3>Why It Matters</h3>
<ul><li><strong>Credit:</strong> Prove you did the heavy lifting. "I designed the logic; AI just typed it."</li>
<li><strong>Safety:</strong> Reviewers can focus their energy on the AI-generated parts (which might be buggy) and trust the human parts.</li>
</ul>
<strong>Key Takeaway:</strong> Prove your human contribution automatically, even for "Tab Coders" using AI assistants.
