TL;DR: We don't collect, store, or transmit any of your data. Everything happens locally on your device.

Overview

Vouch Protocol ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data our browser extension handles and how.

Data We DON'T Collect

  • We do not collect personal information
  • We do not track your browsing history
  • We do not send data to external servers
  • We do not use analytics or tracking
  • We do not sell any data (we have none to sell)

Data Stored Locally

The extension stores the following data locally on your device using Chrome's storage API:

  • Your cryptographic keypair - Generated once, stored only on your device
  • Address Book - Email/key pairs of contacts you've verified
  • Display name - Optional name you set for signatures

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

Permissions Explained

  • identity - To get your Google account email for signatures
  • storage - To save your keypair and contacts locally
  • contextMenus - To add "Sign with Vouch" to right-click menu
  • activeTab - To read selected text on the current page
  • clipboardWrite - To copy signed text to clipboard

Third-Party Services

The extension uses TweetNaCl.js for cryptographic operations. This library runs entirely locally and makes no network requests.

Future Short URLs (Optional)

When enabled, signatures may be stored on our servers to generate short verification links. This feature:

  • Only stores the signature data you explicitly sign
  • Does not store passwords or private keys
  • Allows anyone to verify signatures via short URLs
